# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

Photorealistic studio product photo of a finished tromba marina (trumpet marine): a very tall, slender bowed monochord-family instrument standing taller than a person, with a tapered triangular wooden body that narrows toward a small head at the top and widens toward a broad base, and one long single bowed string running down the front soundboard. Its recognizable identity comes from the asymmetrical buzzing trumpet bridge near the lower string: one foot carries the normal string load while the other foot rests just barely against the soundboard so it can rattle and buzz when bowed. A simple wooden tuning peg at the head and a plain string anchor at the base; the neck or string path carries restrained, subtle reference marks for natural harmonics rather than frets. Warm hand-finished tonewood with visible straight grain and a soft satin historical-workshop varnish, no claim of measured reconstruction. Clean neutral studio background, soft directional studio light, three-quarter product-photography angle, approximately 50mm lens, realistic scale cues from the floor, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

Photorealistic workshop test scene staging a tromba marina buzzing-bridge bench rig beside the tall instrument concept: a single tensioned string stretched over an interchangeable trumpet bridge on a small soundboard coupon, with replaceable shims and contact-material samples laid out on the bench, plus the towering body leaning nearby and a long bow within reach. Natural window light rakes across the soundboard; a musician lightly touches a harmonic node along the string rather than pressing it to a fingerboard. Wood shavings, clamps, and hand tools imply careful prototype work. Shallow depth of field keeps the bridge in focus, warm lived-in environmental storytelling, concept visualization only.

## Detail Prompt

Photorealistic macro close-up of the signature trumpet bridge mechanism on the soundboard: one carved wooden foot firmly seated and bearing string load while the opposite loose foot barely touches the soundboard through a tiny replaceable shim, the single bowed string crossing the bridge crown, and fine optional sympathetic strings softly blurred inside the body as a restrained resonance branch. Raking side light to emphasize relief, realistic wood grain, and the tiny contact and wear point at the loose foot, high detail, no measurements.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake historical/maker labels; no dimension callouts or measurement lines; no fretted guitar necks or multi-string cello layouts; no impossible buzzing-bridge geometry; no extra limbs or distorted hands; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
